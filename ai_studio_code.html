Frequently Asked Questions (SVL Act)

Find quick answers to common questions about the Street Vendors Act and our platform.

The Street Vendors (Protection of Livelihood and Regulation of Street Vending) Act, 2014, is an Indian law enacted to protect the rights of urban street vendors and regulate street vending activities.

TVCs are statutory bodies formed under the SVL Act, responsible for conducting surveys of street vendors, issuing Certificates of Vending (CoV), and identifying vending zones.

A CoV is a license issued by the TVC to street vendors, granting them the right to vend in a designated area and protecting them from harassment and eviction.

By designating vending zones and issuing licenses, SVL helps organize the informal sector, reduce congestion, improve hygiene, and integrate street vendors into the formal urban economy, leading to market stratification.
